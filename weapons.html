Medieval Weapons Of Europe

Swords

Swords becoming longer,lighter, and stronger and led to the the dawn of the famous longswords of the time.

Spears

Versatile, it could be thrown or thrusted, easy to master and reproduce, widely regarded as the first projectile weapon.

Throwing axe

A throwable axe that has a short range, typically used before hand-to-hand confrontation.

Bows

A major advancement in ranged technology, they were cheaper than cavilary, and used a string tied to a curved piece of wood to shoot arrows. Earlier designs had range reaching 100 metres. Having 3 major advancements being long bow, composite bow and crossbow.

Composite Bow The composite bows had a range reaching 100m-275m. They had high power and were really effecient.
Longbows Typically attributed to the welsh, however use has been found in Northern Europe in as early as the dark ages. They are known to reach the ranges of 275-400m. They allowed for massive draw-strength and quick reloads.
Crossbow Originating in China, the crossbow became really common in Europe in the 13th century. First being made of wood which allowed it to be cocked by hand, but later on the crossbows needed more strength, and we saw the dawn off steel crossbows, even needing mechanical components. However that was not the biggest drawback, it had a really long reload speed, but the abilty to pierce armour was a really big strength.
